Legends

Ivan Kudriashov

  • A student of Malevich and Pevzner in the Moscow free workshops, Kudriashov was a leading representative of pictorial “cosmism”.


  • His father manufactured aeronautical devices in the bureau of the scientist and designer Konstantin Tsiolkovsky. Kudriashov helped his father with drawings and models and eventually became interested in the ideas of intergalactic light-air prophecy (cosmism). It can be said that he was the predecessor of James Turrell and Olafur Eliasson.


  • One of his key works is the general scheme of painting of the 1st Soviet theater auditorium in Orenburg. This is an example of how non-objective painting can be combined with real space. Standing in front of the painting with a scjeme, the viewer should imagine that he is lying on the floor of an ancient multi-tiered theater, and then the imaginary arches will turn out to be a painting of tiers, the floor will be a Suprematist curtain, and the red square in the center will be a lampshade.


  • At the end of the 1920s, Kudriashov’s ideas were no longer in demand. The artist was expelled from all artistic associations for “formalism and persistent pointlessness,” but he could not change his principles and preferred to be poor, but not a socialist realist. During this period, he took on any work, for 2 years he was even a prison guard - he was in charge of a storage room belongings of a prison.


  • Kudriashov anticipated Gagarin's flight. Since 1957, he had again turned to the space theme. Inspired by news of the cosmonaut dogs Belka and Strelka, Kudriashov creates a watercolor of a Suprematist cosmonaut stretching his arms in interstellar space.


  • For Kudriashov’s personal exhibition at the Jean Chauvelin gallery in Paris in 1970, works were smuggled out of Russia.
