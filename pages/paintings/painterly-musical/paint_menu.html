Painterly Musical Construction

Mikhail Matiushin

1918, Watercolor and gouache on cardboard

24,8 x 23,8 cm.

Two different kinds of color-forms under the same title are kept at the Costakis collection of MOMus. The two paintings have things in common in composition and in painting technique (pointillism). During the whole of his creative activity Mikhail Matiushin was concerned with the problem of painting space in its essence. Here are two pictures where we see what the philosopher Nikolai Fedorov called “genesis of the fabric” of space. The composition is based on a complex rhythm of twisting, rotating mass. That’s a symbol of the space substance, of its unity and color diversity. Brushstrokes of different size and color create a visual vibration that leads to the effect of shifting. Matiushin and his students often used this method while painting the pure substance of aerial or underwater space. The substance rotation is related to a high musical overtone. It is a kind of celestial music where colors play and interconnect, producing a sound/color vibration.
